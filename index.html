Virat Kohli

"I love the challenge of being the best in the world." - Virat Kohli

Early Life and Career

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. He developed a passion for cricket at a young age and showed exceptional talent early on. Kohli's cricketing journey began when he played for the Delhi Under-15 team and later represented the Under-17 and Under-19 teams.

In 2006, he made his international debut for the Indian cricket team in an ODI match against Sri Lanka. Kohli's impressive batting skills and consistent performances quickly gained attention, and he became a key player in the team.

Achievements and Records

Over the years, Virat Kohli has achieved numerous milestones and set remarkable records. He is the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Kohli also holds the record for the fastest century in ODIs by an Indian player.

In Test cricket, Kohli has been equally dominant, scoring centuries against top-ranked teams and leading the Indian team to significant victories. He has received numerous awards and accolades, including the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions.

Off the Field

Beyond his cricketing achievements, Virat Kohli is known for his philanthropic work and advocacy. He actively supports various causes, including child welfare and education. Kohli has also been involved in campaigns promoting fitness and healthy lifestyles.
